Poverty In Malaysia (1970-2022)

Trends Over Time

Poverty in Malaysia has gradually declined as the nation's economy developed and living standards improved. Despite that, many families continue to experience financial difficulties that prevents them from meeting basic needs.

Poverty can be measured in three ways:
Hardcore Poverty - Living in extreme hardship
Absolute Poverty - Unable to afford basic needs
Relative Poverty - Having Income below average

Distribution Across States and Federal Territories

Poverty trends vary across regions (13 states and 3 federal territories). In earlier years, there are fewer measurements, especially for the federal territories, where records only started in 1984. This is due to some territories being formed later.

Ethnic Poverty

In a multiracial country, comparing ethnic groups highlights which communities face higher poverty. Over 17 years, the Other category continues to experience higher poverty rates, which may include ethnicities such as Malay, Iban, or non-Malaysian citizens.
Poverty rate among Indians has also raised significantly by 2.9%.

Poverty Risk Factor 1: Unemployment

Without a job, people do not have enough income to meet their basic needs, increasing their risk of falling into poverty.

Throughout the years, female unemployment rates have remained higher than male rates, with 2009 being the worst year, as women were more likely to experience poverty due to a poor economy and fewer job opportunities.

Poverty Risk Factor 2: Financial Gap, Income, Expenditure

Income and expenditure plays an important role in determining poverty.

The financial gap refers to the difference between income and expenditure. A wider gap may indicate that some families have higher income thus, lower poverty. However it can also indicate that families are spending more than they earn so increasing their risk of falling into poverty.

One outlier shows a much higher poverty rate than expected based on its income and expenditure levels, suggesting that the state may be experiencing some issues.
